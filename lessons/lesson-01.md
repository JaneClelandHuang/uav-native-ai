# Lesson 1: Course intro; accountable AI-assisted development

## Topic

What this course is, and what it isn't: you are not learning to prompt an
assistant to write code for you and hoping it's right. You're learning a
disciplined engineering workflow — prompting, architecture design, testing
and validation, and systematic review — applied to a real cyber-physical
system (a UAV) with real safety stakes, even in simulation.

The phrase that matters most this semester: **accountable AI-assisted
development**. Every claim you make about your code's quality — "this
works," "this is safe to arm," "this handles the failure case" — needs to be
defended with empirical evidence and an appropriate software engineering
metric, not just "I ran it once and it looked fine."

Also this week: a look at where the semester goes. Three parts — UAV
software components, an agentic decision-making layer, an operator-facing
application — building on top of each other, all against one running
simulation environment rather than three disconnected exercises.
